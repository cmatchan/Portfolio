Research

I am currently a research assistant at Bioinspired Robotics and Design Lab at UC San Diego. Our lab specializes in soft robotics that forgo rigid and electrical components in robotics. The benefit of soft robots lies in their material flexibility as a result of their soft, silicone-based material construction. Rigid robots can be dangerous to workers in a factory environment or patients in a healthcare environment, can damage delicate objects such as fruits or animals, or can be dangerous to operate in gaseous or aqueous environments due to their electrical components. In contrast, soft robots that are powered and controlled by pneumatics can be an excellent solution to these operating scenarios.

My research focuses on the exploration of pneumatic circuit analogs of existing electrical circuit designs. Some projects of mine include designing pneumatic analogs of a potentiometer, a transistor, and an LED grid display. The pneumatic potentiometer project provides an inexpensive dynamic pressure control system for pneumatic actuators. The pneumatic transistor project demonstrates how the bi-stable soft valve design from previous research literature can be used to program the gait of a walking quadruped robot. The pneumatic LED grid display serves as a control system foundation for haptic shape displays that can be used to enhance virtual reality experiences.
